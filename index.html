Overview

The majority of social media content is informal. Furthermore, while discussing Indian social media, users frequently opt to utilize Roman transliterations of their native languages with English embedding, creating a code-mixed scenario. Code-Mixed Information Retrieval (CMIR) is challenging because queries written in Roman or native scripts must be matched to the documents written in the roman script or roman and native script. However, this study will focus only on Roman transliterated Bengali mixed with English Code-Mixed Facebook data. The Roman transliterated version of a Bengali word has no standards and may have multiple spelling variants. Therefore CMIR must solve a non-trivial term matching solution for searching for matching each Roman transliterated query term with the requested word(s). Therefore Information retrieval (IR) on such Indian social media data is a challenging and complex task when the documents and the queries are a mixture of two or more languages written in either the native scripts or the Roman transliterated form. Aside from these concerns, one of the primary reasons for not studying this topic is the scarcity of datasets. As a result, we built a 50 queries dataset with 150k documents. It required a long time and effort to collect data from Facebook and annotate it to build query relevance files. We explain a corpus collection process, discuss the constraints of current state-of-the-art information retrieval systems on such material, and formulate the problem of Code-Mixed Information Retrieval on informal texts.
